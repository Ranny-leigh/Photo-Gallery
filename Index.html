Improv comedy, short for improvisational comedy, is a form of live theater where most or all of the performance is created spontaneously, without a script. Performers rely on quick thinking, collaboration, and audience suggestions to build scenes, characters, and dialogue in real time. The core principle of improv is the concept of "yes, and," which encourages accepting and building on the ideas of others to keep the scene moving forward. This form of comedy thrives on unpredictability, making each show unique and often hilariously unexpected.
